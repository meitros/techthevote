Facebook

Facebook launched a homepage promotion to encourage people to confirm they were registered to vote. They linked to http://vote.usa.gov/ for registration and encourage social sharing with a "registered to vote" status. SF Chronicle reported that California saw a 13X jump in voter registrations on the day of this Facebook promotion.

Google

Google launched a National Voter Registration Day Doodle on their homepage calling upon people to vote in English, Spanish and Chinese within their animated image. They pointed this Doodle at their 2016 Election search tool which gives people all the state-specific info they need to vote. (This data is also available to other companies via Google's Civic Information API.)

Airbnb

Airbnb launched and promoted a voter registration site at http://votingrightsact.airbnbaction.com/ to commemorate the day the Voting Rights Act was signed in 1965. They partnered with National Coalition on Black Civic Participation, Voto Latino and Democracy Works to provide specialized voter registration options, all powered by http://www.turbovote.org.

Square

Square built a feature into its payments platform to allow its many businesses to easily add a "Register Today" call to action at the bottom of their purchase receipts. Their registration prompts took people to http://vote.usa.gov for registration.

Twitter

Twitter launched @Gov to help people register by simply sending a direct message with their zip code. @Gov responds with information about local deadlines and how to register.

The Skimm

TheSkimm launched a site dedicated to the elections at http://www.theskimm.com/2016-election using a white label version of Rock the Vote's tools to power voter registration. They also included links to help people learn more about candidates and election issues. Daily Skimm emails have included promotions pointing to this page. To date, they have helped register over 85K people.

Quora

Quora asked people to register to vote next to their home feed. Their Register to Vote button led people to the National Voter Registration Day site.

Doordash

Doordash partnered with Rock the Vote to add voter registration form delivery to its services. They created a new "store" so customers could "order" registration forms delivered to their doorsteps.
